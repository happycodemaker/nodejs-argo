Data Analytics Methodologies

A strategic framework for transforming raw data into actionable enterprise intelligence.

Modern business decisions rely on robust analytical frameworks. By leveraging different types of analysis, organizations can understand past performance, diagnose operational bottlenecks, forecast future trends, and automate optimal outcomes.

Past Performance

Descriptive Analytics

Answers the question, "What happened?" This methodology consolidates historical data to provide a clear picture of past business outcomes, utilizing data aggregation and mining techniques.

Core Focus Historical trends, KPI tracking, and baseline performance metrics.
Common Tools BI Dashboards (Tableau, Power BI), summary statistics, and annual reports.
Root Cause

Diagnostic Analytics

Answers the question, "Why did it happen?" It drills down into descriptive data to isolate anomalies, identify dependencies, and uncover the root causes of specific behaviors or outcomes.

Core Focus Correlation analysis, data discovery, drill-down processing, and segmentation.
Common Tools Statistical software (R, Python/Pandas), SQL queries, and hypothesis testing.
Forecasting

Predictive Analytics

Answers the question, "What is likely to happen?" By applying statistical models and machine learning algorithms to historical data, it estimates the probability of future trends and behaviors.

Core Focus Trend forecasting, risk assessment, and predictive modeling.
Common Tools Regression models, time-series forecasting, and machine learning frameworks (scikit-learn).
Optimization

Prescriptive Analytics

Answers the question, "What should we do?" This advanced methodology suggests specific action plans and quantifies the potential impact of each choice to maximize desired KPIs.

Core Focus Scenario simulation, resource allocation, and automated decision-making.
Common Tools Linear programming, optimization algorithms, and simulation engines.
